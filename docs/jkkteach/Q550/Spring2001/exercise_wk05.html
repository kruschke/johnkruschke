Chapter 9 concerned itself with two types of models: sequential search and parallel search. Both these types of models are feed forward, in that processing stages only affect subsequent stages, and never affect stages that came before. Moreover, the parallel models assume independence between parallel stages, such that what is happening in one stage cannot affect what is happening in a parallel stage. Contemplate models in which processes can feed back to earlier processes (or to themselves), or cross talk between parallel processes. Could this kind of recurrent or lateral processing model address data of Figure 9.7 (p. 394)? I'm looking for thoughtful discussion, not necessarily fully expressed formalizations fit to data!
