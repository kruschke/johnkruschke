Q550 Models in Cognitive Science, Prof. John K. Kruschke

Exercises, Week 1, Due Tuesday January **, 2001.

Please do the four activities linked below. Each activity involves first participating as a subject in a brief, on-line experiment. Then you examine your data and investigate to what extent a simple model fits your data. Average results from a large number of participants are linked next to each activity below. These average results may help clarify the empirical phenomena being modeled, after you have gone through the experiment yourself.

You will find that the activities have page titles referring to "P335 Cognitive Psychology" instead of "Q550 Models in Cognitive Science." This is because I originally developed the activities for my undergraduate cognitive psychology course. Despite the difference in course number, the four activities serve as an excellent introduction to the more advanced modeling we explore in Q550. You will also find that the titles on the activities refer to "Homework 1," "Homework 2," etc. Please just ignore these numbers (they refer only to P335, not to Q550), and do the activities in the order listed below.

The activities also ask you to complete an evaluation form. This evaluation is not required for Q550, and you do not get extra points for doing an evaluation. You are, however, invited to do the evaluations anyway, because I am always interested in making the activities better.
