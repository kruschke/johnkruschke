#1. Muscle tension:
On p. 755, at the end of the 2nd paragraph, Sternberg states:
Muscle tension (MT) and FP interacted, supporting the conjecture that C is related to response execution: the advantage of a short foreperiod was greater when subjects were permitted to tense their muscles. A related experiment showed that MT was additive with both SQ and MF, further supporting the existence of the three stages and the selective influence of MT on stage C.

We have reviewed some of the effects of five factors, SQ,NA, MF, FP and MT. Ten pairs of factors can be constructed from these five, and in one or more of the five experiments mentioned, nine of these pairs have been examined...

#3. From model-generated data to the model:
The fictitious data in the table at right were generated by a bottleneck/ deferred-processing model as shown in Figure 14.27, p. 791. From these data, you will determine which factors affect which stages. Hint: There are just four possible stages to consider: Task 1 pre-bottleneck, Task 1 post-bottleneck, Task 2 pre-bottleneck, Task 2 post-bottleneck.

The table shows the response times for Task 1 and Task 2 for each combination of factor levels. A factor level of "1" is presumed to yield shorter (faster) RTs than a factor level of "2." That is, "1" is easier and "2" is harder. The factor "Delta T" refers to the stimulus onset asynchrony.

  1. Which factors influence Task 1?

  2. Are the factors that influence Task 1 additive or interactive? Draw the relevant graph to support your claim. From this aspect of the data alone, can you determine whether the factors have their influence before or after the bottleneck, or before or after each other?

  3. Are any of the Task-1 factors perfectly propagated into the Task-2 response times? If so, draw the relevant graph to support your claim. From this aspect of the data, what have you learned about the relative ordering of stages affected by the factors in Task 1? 2?

  4. Which factors directly influence Task 2? (A factor that affects Task 1 might indirectly affect Task 2 by having its influence propagated through the bottleneck. This question is not asking about such indirect influences.)

  5. Which Task 2 factor is post-bottleneck? Confirm your conclusion by showing additivity of that Task-2 factor with Delta T. (Draw the relevant graph.)

  6. Does the remaining Task-2 factor show a subadditive interaction with Delta T? What does this imply about the relative order of the stages influenced by the factors in Task 2?
