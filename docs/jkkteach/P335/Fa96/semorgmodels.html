Semantic Organization: Models and Effects

Some empirical effects regarding semantic organization:

EFFECT Example
Hierarchical
(a.k.a. category size effect) 		"A robin is a bird" is faster to verify than "A robin is an animal"
Typicality "A robin is a bird" is faster to verify than "A penguin is a bird"
Reverse Hierarchical "A chicken is a bird" is slower to verify than "A chicken is an animal"
Relatedness "A bat is a bird" is slower to falsify than "A bat is a plant"
Fan More memorized propositions leads to slower recognition times, when the propositions are not integrable.
