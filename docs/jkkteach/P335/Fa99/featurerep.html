Laboratory Experiment:
E. Gibson, Schapiro & Yonas, 1968. (described on p. 27 of Reed)
What Happened? Explanation?
Stimuli, Procedure, I.V.'s and D.V.:
  • Procedure/Task: Indicate quickly whether two letters shown side by side are the same or different.
  • Stimuli: Upper case, English letters. (Only used C, G, P, R, E, F, M, N, W.)
  • I.V.: Various pairings of letters.
  • D.V.: Response time (RT), in msec.
Results:

RT for correctly responding "different." (Just a couple examples are filled in the table below.)

Representation:
  • Content: Presence or absence of specific features, such as "vertical segment" or "intersection."
  • Format: Simple unordered list, or set.
Process:

Check that the two feature sets are the same.

Example of theory:

G = horizontal straight, open horizontal curve.
W = diagonal right straight, diagonal left straight, cyclic change, symmetry.
Fast RT because a distinguishing feature is quickly found.

P = vertical straight, closed curve, intersection, vertical discontinuity.
R = vertical straight, closed curve, intersection, vertical discontinuity, diagonal left straight.
Slow RT because a distinguishing feature is difficult to find.
