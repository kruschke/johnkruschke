Exam 2 Review Topics
Cognitive Psychology (P335_4330), Prof. Kruschke

(Initially posted Thursday, 12 October, 10:15 am; updated 12:45 pm)

First, be sure to review the instructional goals of weeks five through seven.

Additional Exam Topics:

  • Know the knowledge organization diagram in the syllabus.
  • Know the Brown/Peterson paradigm for measuring the duration of STM.
  • Know the Waugh and Norman experiment that tested whether decay or interference was the dominant cause of forgetting in STM.
  • Know the experiment on STM scanning by Sternberg and serial exhaustive vs. serial self-terminating models.
  • Know the experiment by DeGroot on STM of chess positions by experts or novices.
  • Know about proactive interference (P.I.) and release from P.I.
  • Implication of release from P.I. for STM/LTM distinction.
  • Amnesia.
  • Indirect vs. direct tests of memory.
  • Theories of differences in results between indirect and direct tests of memory: Conceptually-driven vs. data-driven, context retrieval vs. familiarity, and transfer-appropriate processing.
  • Semantic vs. auditory codes in patient P.V. (Reed p.122+)
  • Know the experiment by Craik and Tulving on depth of processing.
  • Know about the levels of processing approach and its problems.
  • Encoding specificity and state dependency.
  • Know the experiment by E. Loftus on the effects of post-event information on eye-witness recognition.
  • Understand the main point of the experiment by Brown et al. (1977) regarding eyewitness identification and exposure to mug shots (p.112+ of Reed).
  • False suppressed memories.
  • Know about the mnemonists Aitken and "S."
