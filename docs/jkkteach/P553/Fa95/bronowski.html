P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Jacob Bronowski (1973), The Ascent of Man
Boston: Little, Brown & Co.
Episode 11: Knowledge or Certainty

THEMES:
  • Science is "blind": fallible human inference about reality.

  • Scientist as artist: theories are inherently incomplete.
    (But art is not science!)

  • Science is personal: of people, by people, touches people.

  • Principle of tolerance.
    (Does not imply utter relativism, that anything goes!)

  • Potential conflict of intellectual/scientific ideas with political ideas.

  • "... think it possible you may be mistaken."

SUMMARY:

Formal statistical methods are a profound intellectual expression of the culture of skepticism. They are a monumental achievement at the apex of human culture.

"A whole conception of culture ... the conception that human knowledge is personal and responsible, an unending adventure at the edge of uncertainty." (p.367)

"Every judgment in science stands at the edge of error, and is personal. Science is a tribute to what we can know although we are fallible." (p.374)
