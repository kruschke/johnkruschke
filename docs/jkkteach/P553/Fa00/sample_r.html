Generating a Sampling Distribution for Pearson r

In this exercise, you'll generate a rough sampling distribution for the Pearson correlation statistic, r. The goal of the exercise is to give you a concrete feeling for where the critical value of the r statistic comes from.

Instead of sampling from a bivariate normal distribution, we will sample from a bivariate uniform distribution, namely, the values of two dice. We imagine that there is an X die and a Y die, and each individual in the population has scores determined by a throw of the two dice. We will draw four individuals from the population for each sample (i.e., N=4). We then compute the correlation of X and Y in the sample. We repeat this process many times, and create a frequency histogram of the sample r values.

In the table below, enter the X and Y values from the values of the dice in your sample. Then compute r from the "computational" formula (p. 97 of Aron and Aron),
r = [N*SUM(X*Y)-SUM(X)*SUM(Y)]
/ [ SQRT(N*SUM(X2)-(SUM(X))2) * SQRT(N*SUM(Y2)-(SUM(Y))2) ]
Or, if you have a calculator that computes r, use your calculator.
