P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Exam 3 (Final Exam) Review topics

Be sure to review the instructional goals of weeks 11-15.

  • Dealing with Non-Normal Data
    • Transforming to normal.
    • Rank-order methods. (Wilcoxon not on exam.)
    • Monte Carlo methods: using data only; using hypothesized population distribution.
    • These methods are applied to t-tests, so you'll need to remember how to do t-tests.

  • Dichotomous Population and Binomial Sampling Distribution
    • Permutations, N choose r, and binomial formula.
    • Doing inference and computing power with binomial distribution.
    • Large N approximations: Normal and Poisson.
    • Power of a binomial test.

  • Multi-Category Population and (Pearson) Chi-Square Sampling Distribution
    • Continuous chi-square vs. Pearson chi-square (definitions).
    • Chi-square applied to "goodness of fit" tests.
    • Chi-square applied to tests of independence.
