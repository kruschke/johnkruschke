P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Regarding Exam 2

The exam is closed-book and closed-notes, but you are encouraged to use a calculator. Tables of critical values and of power are provided separately. There are 100 points possible, with points for each problem as marked. You have 75 minutes; please allocate your time carefully.

The exam will have questions of the following type:

  • Do a t-test (by hand) on two independent groups with unequal N's. You can assume pooled variance.

  • Do an ANOVA (by hand) on three groups with equal N's.

  • Compute the Pearson correlation and test its significance.

  • Know how to interpret SPSS t-test output regarding the test of equal or unequal variances.

  • Know how to compute the approximate power of a t-test.

  • Know how to interpret the SPSS output of a simple regression analysis; in particular, the "coefficients" section.

  • Know how to interpret the syntax files that have been used to generate sampling distributions in SPSS, and their graphs.
