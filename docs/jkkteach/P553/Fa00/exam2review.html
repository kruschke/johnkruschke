P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Exam 2 Review topics
October 30, 2000

Be sure to review the instructional goals of weeks 6-10.

  • t-tests of two independent means:
    • Be able to conduct the test (by hand). Know how to handle unequal sample sizes in the two groups.
    • Know the assumptions of the test.
    • Be able to compute effect size and approximate power (using a table). Also be able to estimate power from SPSS-generated sampling distributions.
  • One Way ANOVA:
    • Be able to conduct the test (by hand). Is ANOVA a one- or a two-tailed test?
    • Know the assumptions of the test.
    • Be able to compute effect size and approximate power. Also be able to estimate power from SPSS-generated sampling distributions.
    • Know about proportion of variance accounted for.
  • Bilinear correlation and regression:
    • Be able to compute correlation and test its significance (by hand).
    • Know the assumptions of the test.
    • Be able to compute effect size and approximate power. Also be able to estimate power from SPSS-generated sampling distributions.
    • Know about proportion of variance accounted for.
  • General Linear Model:
    • Know that t-tests, ANOVA, and linear regression are all cases of the general linear model.
    • Know the general form of the F statistic.
    • In multiple linear regression, know how to interpret the computer output, including the regression equation.
