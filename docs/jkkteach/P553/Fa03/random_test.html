Transformations and the Randomization Test

Suppose you have two groups, with scores as follows:

Group AGroup B
1, 2, 179, 10, 27

Conduct a t-test on the difference of group means. Do you trust the results of the test? Are the assumptions for the tabled value of tcrit satisfied?

What transformation might normalize and homogenize the scores? Try these:

Group A Group B
x 1 2 17 9 10 27
sqrt(x)            
log(x)            
rank(x)            

Do a t-test on the sqrt(x) scores, on the log(x) scores, and on the rank(x). Are the values of t noticeably different than for the original scores?

Is any of the transformations clearly better in terms of symmetrizing and homogenizing the distributions?

Do a randomization test. That is, compute the difference in means for the 20 different ways of sampling 3 scores from the 6 altogether (using the original scores). Make a histogram of the 20 differences of means, and determine the 5% critical value. What is your conclusion from the randomization test?

Now do a randomization test on the rank(x) values. Is the conclusion different than the randomization test on the original values?
