P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Sample Exam 2

The exam is closed-book and closed-notes, but you are encouraged to use a calculator (and you may also bring along the calculator manual). Tables of critical values and of power are provided separately. There are 100 points possible, with points for each problem as marked. You have 75 minutes; please allocate your time carefully.

  1. (45 pts. total):

Suppose we have a null hypothesis that feelings of contentment will not change during the exam.   2. (20 pts. total):

    (a, 10 pts). Test whether the means of the following three groups differ. Use a significance level of 5%.
    Group 1: 4.0, 6.0, 8.0
    Group 2: 13.0, 15.0, 17.0
    Group 3: 6.0, 9.0, 12.0

    (b, 3 pts). What assumptions have you made in conducting the test?

    (c, 2 pts). What is the proportion of total variance accounted for by the difference of group means?

    (d, 5 pts). What is the effect size and approximate power of the test?

  3. (20 pts. total):

    (a, 10 pts). Test whether the means of the following two groups differ. Use a two-tailed t-test, with significance level of .05.
    Group 1: 17.0, 21.0, 24.0
    Group 2: 14.0, 15.0, 16.0, 17.0, 18.0

    (b, 3 pts). What assumptions have you made in conducting the test?

    (c, 2 pts). What is the effect size and approximate power of the test?

    (d, 5 pts). If you were to test the difference between group means using ANOVA instead of a t-test, what would be the value of F, and what would be the relevant degrees of freedom?

  4. (15 pts. total): Draw pictures of population and sampling distributions to illustrate effect size and power for t, F, and r.
