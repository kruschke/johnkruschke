Grading policy. I have been asked to clarify the grading policy: What exactly does the syllabus mean by "grading is on a curve"? It means that one's final grade depends on the central tendency of the class, and so it places all of us into an uncomfortable position of uncertainty. To mitigate the uncertainty, let me report here what the "curve" was last year. What follows is the range of weighted homework plus exam points for each letter grade. Remember that the weighting is 40% homework and 60% exams. There tends to be relatively little variance in homwork scores, so the effective weighting on exams is perhaps a bit higher than 60%. 
(.0000 thru .7499='C')  
(.7500 thru .7999='B-')  
(.8000 thru .8299='B')
(.8300 thru .8599='B+')  
(.8600 thru .9199='A-')  
(.9200 thru 1.000='A')
This year's breakdown will probably be similar, but there is NO implication that these percentage cutoffs will apply exactly this year! To mitigate the uncomfortableness, let me remind you that grades in graduate classes are rarely looked at by future employers (they care about your research); instead, grades in graduate classes are used mostly by your home department for internal tracking of student progress.

Printing files from Zinc. There are two ways to print files from Zinc. (You'll need to do this to get a printout of your Minitab sessions.)

Method 1: Download your file (using ftp) from Zinc to your local machine, and print the file from your local machine as you would any other file.

Method 2: Print the file directly from Zinc to a public printer. You'll need to know the "name" of the printer to do this; the printer in PY 120 is called "py120". The full command from the unix prompt is 

lpr -Ppy120 filename
For further information on printing directly from Zinc, see In Unix, how do I print files, list print jobs, and remove print jobs? and In UNIX, how do I designate my default printer?.
