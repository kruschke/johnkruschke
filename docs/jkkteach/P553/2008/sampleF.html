Generating a Sampling Distribution for F

In this exercise, you'll generate a rough sampling distribution for the F statistic. The goal of the exercise is to give you a concrete feeling for where the critical value of the F statistic comes from.

Instead of sampling from a normal distribution, we will sample from something close to a normal, namely, the sum of two dice. The sum is distributed like a "triangle" from 2 to 12, with a mean of 7. You determined this fact back in Cut Your Losses, Episode 2.

We imagine three identical populations (each determined by the sum of two dice), and we grab a sample of size three from each population. That is, you will roll the pair of dice three times to sample from the first population, then roll the pair of dice three more times to sample from the second population, and finally roll the pair of dice three more times to sample from the third population. From those three sets of three scores, compute the F value. Then repeat many times, and create a frequency histogram of the F values.

In the table below, compute the first two repetitions by hand. After those two repetitions, continue to roll the dice to get new values, but let SPSS compute the means, MS, and F for you. (Each individual "score" is the sum of the two dice in a single roll.)
