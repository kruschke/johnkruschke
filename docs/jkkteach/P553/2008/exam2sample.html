P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Sample Exam 2

This exam is closed-book and closed-notes, but you are encouraged to use a calculator. There are 100 points possible, with points for each problem as marked. Distributed separately will be tables of critical values and power. Please do not write on these tables because they will be re-used for future exams. You have 75 minutes; please allocate your time carefully.

For all inferential tests, assume a two-tailed test with 5% Type I error rate.

    (a, 15 pts) Conduct a t-test on the scores shown at the right. You may assume pooled variance. Can the null hypothesis of equal means be rejected? What are the assumptions of using the critical t values in the table?

    (b, 5 pts) Compute the confidence interval for the difference of means (using the approximation in the textbook and used by SPSS).

    (c, 5 pts) What is the estimated effect size in the population?

    (d, 5 pts) Suppose, in some other data set, that N1=22 and N2=19, and that the effect size was estimated to be 0.53. What is the approximate power of the t-test?

    (e, 5 pts) Suppose you use SPSS to run a t-test of some other data set. The output indicates the following for Levene's test of equality of variances: F=4.041, Sig=0.021. The output also indicates that for equal variances assumed, t=2.83 with Sig=0.036, and that for unequal variances assumed, t=2.11 with Sig=0.171. Are the two groups means significantly different or not (for 95% confidence)? Why?
