Which Sampling Distribution?

The sampling distribution of a sample statistic is determined by the constellation of hypotheses about the population from which the samples are drawn. Shown below is a "decision tree" which branches through various population hypotheses and ends up with corresponding sampling distribution implied by the hypotheses. Below the diagram is the same structure in the form of nested tables.

Not shown in the decision tree below is a precursor decision: Should any null hypothesis significance test (NHST) be used at all? I believe the answer to this question is no, and Bayesian analysis should be used instead. Nevertheless, in the interest of illustrating the conceptual structure of NHST, the decision tree below is provided.

Not all hypotheses we studied are in the tree, and some topics we did not have time for are in the tree. For example, we did not have time to study tests of variance. But we did study cases when there are two dependent variables as in Pearson correlation (continuous variables) and χ2 tests of independence of attributes (nominal variables); these cases are not shown in the decision tree. Could you add branches to the tree to accommodate these cases?
