Statistical Power.

Print this in landscape orientation.

We will roll dice to observe the influence of sample size and population variance on statistical power.

Suppose the "null" hypothesis (i.e., the hypothesis we want to reject) is that the mean value of a die is 3.0. The alternative hypothesis is that the mean value of the die is 3.5. (Of course, our real dice are assumed to be fair, so that their mean value is truly 3.5.)

Please note that this exercise is only a rough approximation to the power computations we've been doing with the normal distribution in the textbook. The population distribution of the die values is not normal, and we have not here considered an alpha level for determining the rejection criterion.

The first exercise examines the probability of rejecting the null hypothesis (i.e., the power) for two different sample sizes.
