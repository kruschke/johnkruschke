Statistical Power and Effect Size for Pearson Correlation

Figure 1 (below) illustrates the concepts of statistical power and effect size for Pearson correlation. The diagram will be explained in detail in lecture. One point to be emphasized is that power lives in the sampling distributions, whereas effect size lives in the population distributions. Notice that the sampling distributions for r are limited to lie between -1 and +1. The effect size is not divided by the standard deviation of the population because the populations are already standardized; their standard deviations are already assumed to be 1.
