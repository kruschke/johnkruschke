4A (2 pts): A researcher wants to know whether the sex of the first child from a couple is independent of the sex of the second-born child from the same couple. The researcher compiles birth records from 100 families and finds the following results: 

                1st born
                 M    F
      2nd    M  30   20
      born   F  20   30
Can the researcher reject the null hypthesis of independence? Show your by-hand computations.

4B (2 pts): The researcher wants to know whether the proportional birth rates of boys and girls are equal. She consulted statistical records of large families and compiled results from 1000 families with 7 children. The researcher counted the number of families with 0 girls, the number with 1 girl, and so on, and found these results: 

    
       r    0   1   2   3   4   5   6   7
     freq  42 125 171 162 162 171 125  42
The researcher compared this obtained frequency distribution against the expected binomial distribution. Can the null hypothesis be rejected?
