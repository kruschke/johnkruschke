P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke, Exam 1

P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Exam 1 Review Topics

Be sure to review the instructional goals of the first five weeks.

  • Ubi dubium ibi libertas:
    • What does that mean?
    • How does it relate to statistics?
  • Measures of central tendency and variability:
    • Know about relative magnitudes of mode, median and mean in skewed distributions.
    • Know how to compute mode, median and mean.
    • Know the definitions of mode, median and mean in terms of the measures of variability that they minimize, and know how to compute those measures of variability.
  • Distribution of the sample M:
    • What does the sampling distribution of the mean look like for different sample sizes and different shapes of population distribution?
    • Be able to state (and understand) the Central Limit Theorem.
    • What is the expected value, variance, and shape of the sampling distribution of the mean?
  • Distribution of the sample z:
    • Know the definition of sample z. That is, know how the sample mean is standarized within its own sampling distribution.
    • Hypothesis testing: Be able to conduct a z-test.
    • Power: For z-tests, know about effect size, sample size, and power.
    • Confidence Interval: Be able to define and compute a confidence interval (2-tailed) based on a z-test.
  • Distribution of the sample t:
    • Know the definition of t.
    • Hypothesis testing: Be able to conduct a t-test.
    • Hypothesis testing: Know why your sample data should be approximately normal if you want to use the usual critical values for t.
    • Power: For t-tests, know about effect size, sample size, and power.
    • Confidence interval: Be able to define and compute a confidence interval (2-tailed) based on a t-test.
