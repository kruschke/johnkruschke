3. (8 pts) A researcher is studying the effects of two drugs. Four rats are randomly assigned to each of two groups, where Group 1 gets Drug A and Group 2 gets Drug B. The researcher measures the time it takes each rat to get through a maze. Results are as follows: Group 1 times (in minutes): 1.000, 0.500, 0.333, 0.250. Group 2 times: 0.250, 0.200, 0.167, 0.143. Conduct a two-tailed t-test on the times. Are the assumptions of the t-test met? Now reconsider the dependent variable. Instead of measuring time taken, convert it to speed. Since the net distance travelled was a constant, converting time to speed is accomplished by computing the reciprocal of time. Now conduct a two-tailed t-test on the speeds. Are the conlusions the same for the test on time and on speed? Which test is more appropriate and why? Finally, convert the scores to rank values, and conduct a t-test on the ranks. How does the conclusion compare with the previous two tests? (End)
