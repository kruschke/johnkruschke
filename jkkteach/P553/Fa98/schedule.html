P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

SCHEDULE

This information is tentative and will be frequently changed and updated. Changes will be announced in class and shown on the web (http://www.indiana.edu/~jkkteach/P553/schedule.html).

Homework is assigned on Tuesdays, and is due the following Tuesday, when another assignment is given (except on exam weeks). This homework schedule is designed so that the Thursday or Friday lab sessions can be partially devoted to discussions of the current week's homework. Do not print out or start a homework assignment until the day it is assigned, because the assignment might change the day before!

  • PART 1, WEEKS 1-5: Models of data, and parameter estimation.

    • Week 1: (Sept. 1, 3)
      • Topic: Syllabus. Computer logistics. Basic concepts of prediction and explanation, types of variables. Overview of course.
      • Reading: Ch. 1 of textbook and study guide.
      • Homework:Textbook p.22 #1, p.34 #3, p.42 #1, #2. Study Guide p.3 #3, #4, p.4 #6, pp.6-8 #1-4. NOTE: Don't just give a one-word answer (as in the back of the book); explain why!
      • Lab: Bring dice and calculator. Cut your losses (p.6 of textbook). Discussion of homework.

    • Week 2: (Sept. 8, 10)
      • Topic: Graphs of data; measures of central tendency and dispersion, standardized scores; model fitting and parameter estimation.
      • Reading: Ch. 2 (all), Ch. 3.1 and 3.2.
      • Homework:Text p.106 # 2, p. 110 #6, p.127 #1, p.134 #1, Study Guide p.13 #1, p.19 #5 and more on the web.
      • Lab: Bring dice and calculator. Cut your losses using D1xD2 (instead of D1+D2), with loss measured by deviation squared, absolute deviation, or presence of deviation. Discussion of homework.

    • Week 3: (Sept. 15, 17)
      • Topic: Models with predictor variables; notion of probability.
      • Reading: Ch. 3.3, 4.1.
      • Homework:Text p.149 #1 (don't turn in Table 3.5 for part a), #2, p.172 #1-4, Study Guide p.29 Short Problems #1-6, Exercise #1, p.34 #1.
      • Lab: Dice rolling (even vs. odd) to demonstrate law of large numbers. Discussion of exercises.

    • Week 4: (Sept. 22, 24)
      • Topic: The central limit theorem (CLT); the normal distribution as a model for residuals. Beyond the textbook: Derivation of binomial distribution. With normal distribution as a model of residuals, then maximal likelihood estimation implies that best parameter values are mean and variance (just like least square estimation).
      • Reading: Ch. 4.2-4.5
      • Homework: Study Guide p.34-36, Short Answer #5, #6, #7, Short Problems #1, #2, #5, #7, #8, and more on the web.
      • Lab: Demo of the CLT by using dice. Discussion of homework.

    • Week 5: (Sept. 29, Oct. 1)
      • Topic: Tuesday review and damage control; Thursday EXAM 1.
      • Reading: Review Ch. 1-4.
      • Homework: Practice Exam 1.
      • Lab: No lab this week.

  • PART 2, WEEKS 6-10: Doubt, confidence, and inference.

    • Week 6: (Oct. 6, 8)
      • Topic: Confidence, doubt, tolerance and freedom. Bronowski video. Confidence intervals for p in the binomial distribution, and for the mean when the population variance is hypothesized.
      • Reading: Ch. 5.1, 5.2.1, 5.2.2.
      • Homework: Textbook: p.223 #1a-c., p.224 #2. Guide: p.41 #10, p.42 #1, #3. And more on the web...

      • Reading: Ch. 5.2.3, 5.3, 6.1, 6.2.
      • Homework: Textbook: p.239 #1, p.240 #2a (not 2b), p.250-251 #1, 2, 3, p.273 #1, 2. Guide: p.43 #9, 10, p.51 #3. And more on the web...

    • Week 8: (Oct. 20, 22)
