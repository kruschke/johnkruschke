P553 Statistics in Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Exam 1 Review Topics

Be sure to review the instructional goals of the first five weeks.

  • Measures of central tendency and variability:
    • Know about relative magnitudes of mode, median and mean in skewed distributions.
    • Know how to compute mode, median and mean.
    • Know the definitions of mode, median and mean in terms of the measures of variability that they minimize, and know how to compute those measures of variability.
  • Sampling distribution of the sample mean:
    • What does the sampling distribution look like for different sample sizes?
    • What is the Central Limit Theorem?
    • What is the central tendency, variance, and shape of the sampling distribution of the mean?
  • z-tests:
    • For z-tests, know about effect size, power and sample size, and be able to conduct a z-test (and know when to do so; see the decision tree for tests considered so far).
  • t-tests:
    • For t-tests, know about effect size, power and sample size, and be able to conduct a t-test (and know when to do so; see the decision tree for tests considered so far). Know about single-sample t-tests and paired-samples t-tests.
