Exam 2 Review Topics, Updated 14 October 1996

Essay Question Topics:

  • The Peterson and Peterson (aka Brown/Peterson) paradigm for measuring the duration of STM.
  • The Waugh and Norman experiment that tested whether decay or interference was the dominant cause of forgetting in STM.
  • The experiment on STM scanning by Sternberg and serial-exhaustive vs. serial self-terminating models.
  • The experiment by DeGroot on STM of chess positions by experts or novices.
  • Proactive interference (P.I.) and release from P.I., and the implication for STM/LTM distinction.
  • The experiment by Craik and Tulving on depth of processing.
  • The experiment by E. Loftus on the effects of post-event information on eye-witness recognition.

Multiple Choice Question Topics:

  • Encoding specificity and state dependency.
  • Semantic vs. auditory codes in patient P.V. (Reed p.148+)
  • Indirect vs. direct tests of memory (Reed pp.136-140)
  • The mnemonist, Professor Aitken.
  • Understand the main point of the experiment by Brown et al. (1977) regarding eyewitness identification and exposure to mug shots (p.112+ of Reed).
  • Amnesia - its definition and characteristics.
  • Confabulated vs. veridical suppressed memories.
  • Know about the levels-of-processing approach and its criticisms.
