Exam 1 Review Topics, Updated 18 September 1996

Essay Question Topics:

  • Kruschke's definition of cognitive psychology.
  • What is in any theory of a cognitive phenomenon?
  • Examples of representation and process, such as for adding numbers or looking up a phone number.
  • Laboratory procedures for evaluating feature theories of pattern recognition.
  • The word-superiority effect and the interactive-activation model.
  • Neiser and Becklen experiment regarding attention to events.
  • The Stroop task, its results, and explanation. What's an "automatic" process?
  • What makes a person a good fighter pilot or bus driver? What laboratory procedure measured that?

Multiple choice question topics:

  • Know the knowledge organization diagram in the syllabus.
  • Evidence for templates in human pattern recognition.
  • How do most computers or robots recognize patterns?
  • Feature theories of pattern recognition. What laboratory procedures are used to evaluate them?
  • Hierarchical cluster diagrams (in relation to feature theories of pattern recognition; p.27 of Reed).
  • Bottom-up vs. top-down context effects, and examples of each.
  • Interactive-activation model of context effects: How it explains the word-superiority effect and face priming.
  • Sperlings's and Rumelhart's models of performance in the whole-report procedure (pp.35-38 of Reed).
  • Pre-attentive and attentive levels of perception.
  • Role of attention in vision and audition (what is attention for, what does it do).
  • Illusory conjunctions.
  • Broadbent's filter theory of attention and why it is incomplete.
  • Understand bottleneck and capacity theories of attention.
  • Attention to events: How did Neiser and Becklen demonstrate this?
