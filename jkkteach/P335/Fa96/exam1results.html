Exam 1 Results

One recurring confusion that appeared in exam answers was failure to carefully distinguish between results and theory. For any experiment, a certain procedure yields particular measurable results or effects. These results are the data, the facts of the matter. How to explain these results is something else entirely. Many different theories might account for the results. Any detailed theory must specify the representation and processing of information. But the representation and processing is in the theory, not in the mind. The theory is supposed to formally express our understanding of the mind, but is not to be confused with the real mind in the real world. Thus, experimental results are factual data, not possible theories. Explanations are provided by theories, but theories are not data; theories try to account for data.
