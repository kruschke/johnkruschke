P335 Cognitive Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Updated 9:45 am, Monday 2/2/98

Exam 1 Review Topics

Essay Question Topics:

  • Kruschke's definition of cognitive psychology. What is in any theory of a cognitive phenomenon?
  • Laboratory procedures for evaluating feature theories of pattern recognition; specifically Gibson's experiment and theory, pp.24-28.
  • The word-superiority effect and the interactive-activation model; pp.39-43.
  • What makes a person a good fighter pilot or bus driver? What laboratory procedure measured this? (pp. 71-73)

Multiple choice question topics:

  • Know the knowledge organization diagram in the syllabus.
  • Evidence for templates in human pattern recognition, specifically Phillips experiment, pp. 21-24.
  • How do most computers or robots recognize patterns?
  • Feature theories of pattern recognition. What laboratory procedures are used to evaluate them? (pp. 24-29)
  • Hierarchical cluster diagrams (in relation to feature theories of pattern recognition; p.27 of Reed).
  • Bottom-up vs. top-down context effects, and examples of each.
  • Interactive-activation model of context effects: How it explains the word-superiority effect and face priming. (pp.39-46)
  • Sperlings's and Rumelhart's models of performance in the whole-report procedure (pp.35-38 of Reed).
  • Pre-attentive and attentive levels of perception. (pp. 51-58 and lectures regarding feature-integration theory)
  • (Unlimited capacity) parallel and (self-terminating) serial search processes in visual search tasks.
  • Role of attention in vision and audition (what is attention for, what does it do, in seeing and hearing).
  • Illusory conjunctions.
  • Broadbent's filter theory of attention and evidence that it is incomplete. (pp. 51-58)
  • Understand bottleneck and capacity theories of attention. (especially summary, pp. 61-62)
  • Attention to events: How did Neisser and Becklen demonstrate this?
  • The Stroop effect and its explanation in terms of automatic processes (pp. 64-65)
