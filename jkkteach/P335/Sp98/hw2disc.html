> Prof. Kruschke, On homework 2, I was thinking that, on the Probe
> Present charts for self-terminating scan and exhaustive scan of 1
> item, there wouldn't be the question of "another item" since one
> item is present.  This would subtract 10 sec from those charts.
> Would the absent charts also not need the another item time since
> there is only 1 probe also?  Please help me.  Thanks for your time.

Yes, you're right, at least for self-terminating search with the probe present. In self-terminating search, once the probe is found, then there is no checking of further items, so the last time through, when the probe is present, there is no addition of 10 msec for "Another Item?".

When the probe is absent, self-terminating search continues through all the items, and checks for another item after every item checked. So for absent probes, the 10 msec for "Another Item?" does get added for every item.

In exhaustive scan, the loop continues through all the items, even after the probe is found in the memory set, and so the time for checking "Another Item?" is included every time.

When writing the homework assignment, I simply hadn't thought of this subtlety for self-terminating search with probe present, and so the homework is unintentionally misleading. For grading purposes, we'll accept either answer.

Curiously, this same problem was present on Homework 1, which also involved self-terminating serial search (in vision), but nobody noticed (or at least nobody who noticed told me about it). This time, you and several other people noticed. To me this is a good sign: You're really learning and understanding the details!
