The course schedule is divided into four parts:

  • Part I: Ambiguity in Perception
  • Part II: Uncertainty in Memory
  • Part III: Ambiguity in Interpretation
  • Part IV: Uncertainty in Reasoning

PART I: AMBIGUITY IN PERCEPTION
Week Date Ch. Topic Due or Assigned
1 Tu 1 Sept 1 Syllabus and goals of the course. Definition of cognitive psychology: Cognitive psychology explains mental phenomena primarily in terms of information processing, only secondarily in terms of neural processing. All well specified theories posit particular representations and processes.
Th 3 Sept 2 Pattern recognition: Perception is an inference, a construction, from ambiguous sensory information (even perception of cinnamon rolls). Theories of pattern recognition based on different representations: templates, features and structures.
2 Tu 8 Sept 2 Pattern recognition: Bottom-up (image driven) and top-down (knowledge driven) context effects. Use of context in recognition is usually a very effective way to reduce ambiguity in interpreting an image.
Th 10 Sept 2 The partial-report procedure and visual attention: Iconic memory has large capacity and fades rapidly, acts as a buffer for directing attention. The role of attention in pattern perception: feature integration. An example of lack of feature integration in unattended items: a card trick.
3 Tu 15 Sept 2,3 Visual search paradigm and feature integration theory of attention. Attention to locations, objects and events. Homework 1 (template model) DUE at beginning of class.
Th 17 Sept 3 Discussion of proposals. Bottleneck and capacity perspectives on attention. Relevance paper PROPOSAL 1 DUE at beginning of class.
4 Tu 22 Sept 3 Bottleneck and capacity perspectives on attention (cont.). Attention in bus drivers and fighter pilots. Relevance PAPER 1 DUE at beginning of class.
Th 24 Sept 1-3 EXAM 1 Evaluate Homework 1.

PART II: UNCERTAINTY IN MEMORY
Week Date Ch. Topic Due or Assigned
5 Tu 29 Sept 4 Short-Term Memory (STM): Duration; Forgetting as interference vs. decay; Capacity and chunking. STM capacity in novices and experts.
Th 1 Oct 4-5 Expertise might be only practice. Scanning of STM: data and models. Acoustic-semantic distinction in STM vs. LTM: independent manipulation of primacy and recnecy effects.

Imagery in memory: Huge capacity; imagery and the mnemonist; imagery and advertising. Homework 2 (attention shifts in learning and memory) DUE at beginning of class.
Th 15 Oct 7 Discussion of proposals. Imagery and memory: Context bound imagery; Reality monitoring. Imagistic vs. symbolic representation. Maze running demo. Parallel processing of images vs. serial processing of symbols. Relevance paper PROPOSAL 2 DUE at beginning of class.
8 Tu 20 Oct 7 Properties of imagery: rotation, scanning, distortions. Imagery and perception: similarities in behavior; interference; neural substrates. Relevance PAPER 2 DUE at beginning of class.
