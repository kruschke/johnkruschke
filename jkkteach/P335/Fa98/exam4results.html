Exam 4 Results

Exam 4 turned out to be considerably more difficult than the previous exams, probably because it relied more on book reading than on lecture material. We therefore adjusted everyone's score so that the distribution of scores would be more similar to the first three exams. The adjustment was done as follows: We took your raw point score from exam 4, multiplied by 1.1, and added 1. This reflects influence of two theories of psychological difficulty; i.e., both multiplicative and additive effects of difficulty. The scores you see in Post'Em are the final, adjusted scores. The class statistics below are also based on the final, adjusted scores.
