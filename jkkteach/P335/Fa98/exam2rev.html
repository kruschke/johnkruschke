Exam 2 Review Topics

Essay Question Topics:

  1. The experiment by DeGroot on STM of chess positions by experts or novices (lecture, Reed pp.88-90).
  2. The experiment by Craik and Tulving on depth of processing (lecture demo, Reed pp.154-157).
  3. The experiment by E. Loftus regarding the effects of post-event information on eye-witness recognition (lecture).
  4. Know how imagery can explain the results of the Shepard and Metzler mental rotation experiment (lecture, Reed pp.197-199).

Multiple Choice Question Topics:

  1. The Peterson and Peterson (a.k.a. Brown/Peterson) paradigm for measuring the duration of STM (lecture demo, Reed pp.81-82).
  2. The Waugh and Norman experiment that tested whether decay or interference was the dominant cause of forgetting in STM (lecture, Reed pp.82-84).
  3. The experiment on STM scanning by Sternberg and serial-exhaustive vs. serial self-terminating models (lecture, Reed pp.104-107).
  4. Semantic vs. auditory codes in patient P.V. (Reed pp.148-149).
  5. The levels-of-processing approach and its criticisms (Reed pp.157-164).
  6. Distinctiveness in memory (Reed pp.161-164).
  7. Encoding specificity and state dependency (lecture, Reed pp.165-169).
  8. The cognitive interview (lecture [in passing], Reed pp.128-129, box 5.1 on pp.130-131).
  9. Transfer appropriate processing (Reed pp.169-170).
  10. Fabricated vs. veridical suppressed memories (lecture, Reed pp.212-214).
  11. The mnemonist, "S." (lecture).
  12. Interactive imagery in advertising (lecture, Reed pp.178-180).
  13. Three themes of memory, and examples of each (lecture).
  14. Scanning of mental images (Reed pp.192-194).
  15. Alignment and rotation heuristics in cognitive maps (lecture).
  16. Know the Smith & Nielson face matching experiment, and how its results were explained (lecture, Reed pp.194-196).
  17. Interference between imagery and perception (lecture, Reed pp.199-204).
  18. "Reality monitoring" (lecture, Reed pp.210-214).
