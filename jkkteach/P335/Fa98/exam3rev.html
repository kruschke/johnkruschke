P335 Cognitive Psychology, Prof. Kruschke

Exam 3 Review Topics

Reading for this exam includes Chapters 8, 9, 10 and 11.

Essay Question Topics:

  1. How concept learning and scientific method are, or are not, analogous (lecture and web notes).
  2. Know how the ACT model accounts for the fan effect (lecture, web notes, and Reed pp.273-275).
  3. Difficulty of completing sentences with different types of ambiguities (Reed pp. 302-304).
  4. Individual differences in resolving text ambiguities and Carpenter and Daneman's (1981) model for reading individual words (Reed pp.309-312).

Multiple Choice Question Topics:

  1. Strategies, such as conservative focussing, in discovering relevant attributes for conjunctive rules (lecture, see also Reed pp.224-225).
  2. Know what types of concept rules are easiest and hardest to learn (Reed pp.221-224).
  3. Shared features among instances of super-ordinate, basic, and sub-ordinate categories (lecture and Reed pp.227-229).
  4. Basic level categories and average shape of instances (Reed pp.230-231).
  5. Family resemblance and typicality ratings (lecture and Reed pp.231-234).
  6. Prototype effects observed by Posner and Keele (1968; pp.241-243, Fig. 8.7).
  7. Brain damage and category loss (Reed pp. 235-239).
  8. Models of categorization (homework, lecture, web notes, and Reed pp.239-248).
  9. The hierarchical (or category size) effect, the reverse hierarchical effect, the typicality effect, the relatedness effect, and the fan effect (lecture, web notes, and Reed pp.262-278).
  10. Similarities and differences among the four models of semantic organization (lecture, web notes, and Reed pp.264-278).
  11. Schematization of memory (Reed pp.278-280).
  12. ELIZA and the Turing test (lecture and web links [see schedule page]).
  13. Spoonerisms and other slips of the tongue, implications for the structure of speech, and Dell's model (lecture and pp. 297-298).
  14. Chomsky's theory of transformational grammar (pp. 300-302) and MacKay's (1966) experiment on sentence ambiguity (pp. 302-304).
  15. Implications in advertising (pp. 317-318).
  16. The role of schemas and scripts in language comprehension and memory (lecture and pp. 326-335).
  17. Perceptual span in reading; maximal reading speed (lecture).
  18. Aphasic comprehension of the president's speech (lecture).
